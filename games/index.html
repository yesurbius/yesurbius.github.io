What makes a successful game?

There are many things that can make or break a video game, but I have made a list of items that I feel contribute to a game being successful.

Character Development

Games that provide the player with a character they can develop are more successful. When a character is developed over time, the player becomes more invested in that character, and by extension, the game. This discourages the player from quitting and starting over fresh, and helps weather the more difficult or tedious parts.

Tech / Skill Templates

Tech / Skill templates, or "builds" give the player the ability to choose a progression path in the game. When a player is successful in a game, they attribute the success to the build unique choices the player made in the game, not the game mechanics or balancing. Players will share their builds with other players, and seek to try out new build designs. In some cases, trying the various builds becomes a game within itself. When the game is altered based on the techs / skill choices of the player, then game's replayability is greatly increased.

Open World Exploration

Allow the player to explore the world the game has to offer, instead of locking the player into specific zones. Open world exploration lets the player "discover" the game instead of being fed it. It allows the player to progress at their own pace and choose their own progression paths.

Simplicity

Simple controls that are easy to understand and master are essential to any successful game. Simple camera modes that do not get in the player's way make the game successful. A simple backstory. History has shown us that the simpler games go on to be the greatest. This does not mean that games cannot be complex, but they should be complex in their content, which the player consumes, not complex to use.

Rewards

Keep that dopamine flowing! Gold coins. Flashing lights. Happy audible chimes. Words of encouragement. Read any modern gamification article and you will see that rewards are key to any successful game. It keeps the player hooked, keeps the player happy, and encourages the player to play more.

Quests / Missions

There is nothing worse than a great game that you can't figure out what to do. Quests and missions are integral to a game for two reasons.

  1. They keep the player busy with new challengs and things to do
  2. They direct the player through the game. This can include ensuring the player knows how to play (a tutorial), or it can include the game objectives.

Learning / Skill

Humans love to learn and acquire new skills. Games can achieve this in one of two ways. The first is by educating them and the second is by making the game a skill.

Like any good book, the author should spend a fair bit of time researching the material in their game in order to ensure that their game achieves suspension of disbelief. You want the player to "just go with it" and not pause gameplay and cry foul.

The other method of invoking learning is to make the game itself a skill. When the game itself is a skill, it means that the more you play - the better you get, regardless of your character. In Super Mario Bros the player's skill at platforming made them a better player. That skill takes practice to learn and master. Much the same as the character development point - the player becomes more invested in a game where skills matter.

Combine both Learning and Skill building for a phenomenal game. Take Kerbal Space Program as an example. Anyone can start the game and launch rockets into space. Those that play the game again and again begin to develop a skill at launching vessels efficiently. But the game's physics is based on real world rocket science. Therefore a player that learns the rocket science will get more from the game and become a better player.

Collecting and Accessories

Humans love to collect. It keeps the game fresh, lets them use the creative parts of their brains and gives them something to impress their friends with.

It has been common place in games for digital items to be sold for real cash. This is a great revenue stream for game developers, but it also keeps the player playing. Why? On the one hand, the player has invested a lot into the game. On the other hand, the game is changing with each accessory.

Fun

Humans love to laugh and have fun. Games that are fun or include elements of comedy will always be more successful.

Brands

Using a familiar brand with your video game establishes a familiarity with the subject matter going in, and certain prebuilt enthusiasm toward the game.
